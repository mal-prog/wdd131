Soft Hats for Patients – Site Plan

WDD 131 – Personal Web Application

Purpose & Audience

Purpose

The purpose of the Soft Hats for Patients website is to share our nonprofit project that creates comfortable, customized hats for patients going through medical treatments. The site will make it easy for visitors to learn about the mission, view hat styles, and see how they can donate or get involved. It will also act as a simple “hub” for managing requests and showcasing impact stories.

Audience

The primary audience includes family members, friends, and caregivers of patients who want to bring comfort and encouragement through small, thoughtful gifts. A secondary audience includes hospital staff, volunteers, and donors looking for meaningful service opportunities. Visitors will likely be emotionally invested, time-conscious, and using the site on phones, tablets, and laptops.

Dynamic Elements (JavaScript)

JavaScript will be used to make the site feel interactive, responsive, and more helpful than a simple static brochure site. Planned dynamic features include:

  • Interactive Hat Gallery: Filter hats by color, theme (e.g., “cheerful”, “calm”, “sporty”), or size using buttons or dropdowns. JS will show/hide hat cards based on the selected filters.
  • Donation Impact Calculator: A small form where users can enter a dollar amount or number of hats. JavaScript will calculate and display how many patients could be helped and show a friendly message (e.g., “Your donation could comfort 5 patients this month!”).
  • Testimonial / Story Slider: A rotating carousel of patient or volunteer quotes that automatically slides every few seconds, with “previous/next” buttons controlled by JS.
  • Theme Toggle (Optional Stretch): A light/dark mode toggle that lets users switch the color theme. JavaScript will apply different CSS classes and remember the choice with localStorage.
  • Form Validation: Basic JavaScript validation for the contact / request form (required fields, email pattern, character limits) to improve user experience.

Logo

The logo will be simple, warm, and friendly. It will likely include a stylized soft hat icon combined with a small heart or ribbon to represent care and support for patients.

Style Guide

Color Palette

The color scheme should feel soft, calm, and hopeful. The planned palette is:

  • Primary: Soft teal – #4FB3BF
  • Accent: Warm coral – #F76C6C
  • Background: Soft cream – #FFF7EC
  • Dark Text / Headings: Slate – #1F2933
  • Muted Gray: Light gray – #E5E7EB

Typography

Headings and key titles will use a friendly, modern sans-serif font such as Poppins or Quicksand. Body text will use a clean, highly readable font such as Open Sans or Roboto with fallbacks to standard system fonts.

  • Headings: “Poppins”, system sans-serif
  • Body: “Open Sans”, system sans-serif

In the final project, Google Fonts will be included in the <head> of the HTML pages.

Content Outline

Below is the planned content for each page of the Soft Hats for Patients site. Text can be refined later, but this provides a clear idea of what will appear where.

Home Page

  • Hero section: Short headline, one-sentence mission statement, and a call-to-action button (“See the Hats” or “Get Involved”).
  • Quick explanation: 2–3 short sentences explaining why soft hats matter to patients and how this project started.
  • Highlight cards: Three small sections such as “Comfort”, “Confidence”, and “Connection”, each with a brief description.
  • Preview of hat gallery: A small set of featured hats with images and one-line descriptions.
  • Testimonial slider: Rotating quotes from patients, family members, or volunteers.

Hat Gallery Page

  • Filters: Buttons or dropdowns for color, size, or theme (e.g., “Bright & Cheerful”, “Soft & Calm”).
  • Hat cards: Each card includes a photo, hat name, short description, and tag(s).
  • Favorites (optional): A “heart” or “save” icon to mark favorite hats (stored locally in the browser).

Get Involved Page

  • Donation Impact Calculator: A form where users can type an amount or number of hats and see the estimated number of patients helped.
  • Ways to Help: Cards or list items describing donating materials, sewing hats, delivering hats, or sharing the project on social media.
  • Contact / Request Form: Fields for name, email, role (patient, family, volunteer, donor), and a message.

About / Our Story Page (Optional)

  • Origin Story: How the idea for Soft Hats for Patients started and why it matters.
  • Mission & Values: Short bullet points on dignity, comfort, and kindness.
  • Photos: A few warm, respectful images showing hats, materials, or (if available and appropriate) patients/volunteers.
